GOA

1. Baga Beach

Baga Beach, one of Goa’s most popular beaches, is known for its vibrant nightlife, water sports, and beautiful sunsets. It offers parasailing, jet-skiing, and beach clubs, making it perfect for both relaxation and adventure. The lively atmosphere attracts tourists year-round, especially for its beach shacks and parties.

2. Old Goa

Old Goa is a UNESCO World Heritage site, famous for its historic churches and colonial architecture. Key attractions include the Basilica of Bom Jesus, St. Augustine Tower, and Se Cathedral. The area's rich history and religious significance make it a must-visit for culture and history enthusiasts.

3. Dudhsagar Waterfalls

Dudhsagar Waterfalls, located on the border of Goa and Karnataka, is one of India’s tallest waterfalls. Surrounded by lush forests, the cascading waters resemble milk, giving the waterfall its name ("Dudh" meaning milk). It’s a popular destination for trekking, jeep tours, and nature lovers looking for adventure.
