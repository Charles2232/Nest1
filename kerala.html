KERALA

1. Alleppey (Alappuzha)

Alleppey, known as the "Venice of the East," is famous for its tranquil backwaters. Visitors can enjoy houseboat cruises along the palm-lined waterways, passing through lush landscapes, traditional Kerala villages, and scenic paddy fields. The serene atmosphere makes it a perfect destination for relaxation and exploration.

2. Munnar

Munnar, a hill station in the Western Ghats, is renowned for its lush tea plantations, misty hills, and diverse wildlife. The Eravikulam National Park and Mattupetty Dam offer scenic views and opportunities for trekking, while the rolling hills and cool climate make it a popular getaway for nature lovers.
