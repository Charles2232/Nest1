JAMMU AND KASHMIR

1. Dal Lake, Srinagar

Dal Lake, known for its scenic beauty, offers shikara rides, houseboats, and vibrant floating markets. Surrounded by Mughal gardens and snow-capped mountains, it’s an iconic destination. Visitors can explore floating gardens, take scenic boat rides, and enjoy the serene atmosphere of Srinagar’s most famous attraction.

2. Gulmarg

Gulmarg, a stunning hill station, is famous for its winter sports like skiing and snowboarding. The Gulmarg Gondola, one of the highest cable cars in the world, offers spectacular views of the Himalayas. During summer, it transforms into a green paradise with alpine meadows, making it a year-round destination.

3. Vaishno Devi, Katra

The Vaishno Devi Temple in Katra is one of India’s holiest pilgrimage sites, attracting millions of devotees annually. Nestled in the Trikuta Mountains, the temple is reached via a scenic trek, offering panoramic views. The spiritual journey to the temple is both a physical and devotional experience.
